General Information

The /mlp/ Secret Santa (5+ years running) is an annual Holiday celebration and gift exchange.

What started in 2015 as a spontaneous exchange on /mlp/ with no more than 80 anons has since grown to a yearly event with over 300+ participants!. The event typically runs through October-January with the bulk of it centering around December. It fosters friendship and goodwill towards fellow pony fans as participants send each other letters, greeting cards, gifts, and care packages. We take this time out of the year to stop being so horrible to one another and embrace our mutual love of tiny horses.

If you would like to participate (whether you want to exchange just cards, a package, or both) click the sign up link in the navigation bar above! The forms are pretty self-explanatory. After signups you will be randomly assigned both a gifter and a giftee. Remember, you’ll need to send a gift to get one back. Failure to do so bars you from future events.

    /ss/ FAQ's


    Is there a price limit?

    The $20-25 minimum (which does not include the cost you should set aside for shipping) is not a hard number perse but more a guideline of the value a gift should have. It can be one big item or multiple small items. If your anon is okay with handmade goods, the perceived value of the handmade item should feel on-par with that amount. You don’t have to stress about hitting the exact number, just put heart and care into it! It’s always a great idea to include small inexpensive items like stickers, cards, letters, etc with your gift to give it a personal touch while remaining within your budget and don’t forget to send a note or letter! If you opt to send items direct from an online shop please please please have the company/shop owner send along a personalized note for your anon. Most if not every vendor should be happy to do this for you.

    How do gifts work?

    Gifts are exchanged round-robin style. Imagine anons sitting in a circle with each anon passing a gift to the person on their right. A gives to B gives to C gives to A. The person assigned to gift you is not the same person you will be shopping for! When assignments go out you will receive all the info your anon filled out in the signup form, like their favorite ponies and preferences.

    You will also receive a unique Holiday ID name for yourself, and one for your assignment so that you can use it when posting in thread during unboxings or to communicate if need be. Have fun with it but understand this is not a free ticket to namefag shamelessly, please use your best judgement when posting with your nickname! Once you have your assignment it’s time to get shopping! Please keep your receipt(s) and tracking information for any and all packages if you are exchanging a gift! Always be sure to alert SantaPone with the tracking information when you ship. The shark typically only needs to know the tracking number and carrier/website to use it with when you send a package from you to your Santee or if you are mailing them items directly from the manufacturer.

    Can I send food?

    If your anon is okay with it! It's suggested to stick to prepackaged items because >4chan but some anons like baking cookies or other treats. Be sure to thoroughly package anything you send to avoid crumbs or mess getting on to any other items. Avoid things that could melt or are easily crushed unless they are in well sealed containers. Be mindful of your giftees listed allergies or preferences as well as any customs regulations if sending internationally.

    Can I send alchohol?

    Domestically, this is not supported through USPS but possible through other mail carriers, so do so at your discretion. Just be sure you wrap it securely and in a separate container in case of leaks or it breaking in transit.

    Non-alcoholic liquids/drinks are fine to send through USPS but you should always package them with extreme care and let the postal worker know your package contains liquids. Internationally, it's not suggested to send any alcohol or liquids. Always be sure to heavily research the customs laws for the country you are shipping to before sending any food or drink. Consider sending non-liquid items such as tea, coffee, hot cocoa mix, etc if customs allows.

    Of course, it's encouraged not to send anything hazardous, medicinal, or outright illegal to posses. Do not send bodily fluids of any kind.

    Privacy

    Here at SantaPone Central, we understand that privacy is an incredibly serious business. A bunch of folks from 4chan giving their information to other anons is a HUGE exercise in trust, and security, credential protections, and authorization have been the absolute top focus of this whole project. As part of the development of this project, I want to be as transparent as I can with each and every one of you about the tech stack used, how it is used, and how your data is protect to the highest degree.

    Service Infrastructure

    Overview

    SantaPone Central is a full stack application with an ASP.NET Web API backend, interfacing Auth0 for a stable and secure role-based management system, and Twilo Sendgrid as a privately used Azure service of its own to have a secure way to deliver notifications and other information to users. The front end is an auth0 manged Angular Single-Page Application itself that utilizes best practice to make for a simple and effective way for clients and event admins to manage and contribute to the event.

    Back End

    There are namely 3 parts to the backend infrastructure; the data service, the email service, and the auth0 service. All of these work together to ensure that data is valid, secure, and stateless. A privately hosted Twilo Sendgrid solution is used to ensure that emails from the API are uniform, and specific to events, assignments, and correspondence to notify users of assignments, correspondence, or any other things that need to be sent, such as password reset requests. The Auth0 service itself is used by utilizing the Auth0 management API itself, ensuring data security and proper credential handling. Being that it as API running on it's own domain, requests are only allowed to come from this site, and the Auth0 tenants used in the data security layer. This specifically has been rigorously tested for fidelity to ensure data security management.

    Front End

    The front end site you see before you was made with the Angular framework to deliver a nice, Single-Page Application web experience. I personally am a huge fan of Angular and the way type-safety and services in-application can be accessed and handled. SantaPone Central is hosted on its own app platform service separate from the API to ensure that data calls and services are exclusively handled on the API layer, and features data protection layers and services by Auth0 to manage user authorization and requests.

    Sendgrid

    Twilo Sendgrid is one of the services hosted privately within Azure. Rather simply, it is the service that allows the API to send secure emails about notifications and correspondence in general.

    Hosting

    The hosting of all of these services are hosted within Azure. If you are not familiar with service architectures, in short, similar services would be Amazon Web Services and CloudFlare. These are secure and expandable cloud computing solutions that allow for a safe and consistent running environment. I use Azure specifically because it is what I am most familiar with, and it interfaces well with a lot of the other Microsoft-based services and frameworks like SendGrid, or ASP.NET in general.

    Auth0

    What exactly is Auth0?

    Auth0, in short is a security service that handles client login information and security

    How secure is it?

    To put it mildly, this is a security platform that AMD, Atlassian, and BlueCross BlueShield themeselves use. It provides a secure way to attach validation to requests that are made with roles that define information about a user, allows for a safe API to manage password changes, and ensures that quite literally nothing gets past the security layer that shouldn't.

    Passwords

    For the sake of security, there are literally no passwords that are or ever will be stored on the database for SantaPone Central. All user credentials and security information is handled exclusively by a privately paid and managed Auth0 tenant by yours truly to make sure every single one of your data is secure. Passwords, to that end, the only people who can even see or change your password is... yourself. Not even myself can see or know your passwords.

    Data

    What data is stored?

    The goal of SantaPone Central is to literally keep the same process to the /SS/ event in a managed, easy-to-use way. That being said, the only things that are stored on the database is the information that has always been asked for. Your names, emails, address, and survey questions are the only things that are asked for upon signing up, and are vigorously and strictly locked behind auth0 for peace of mind and protection.

    Who can access my data?

    Auth0 allows for role-based authorization. That being said, the only people who can view your data are the admins, and the people who are set to send to yourself as an assignment. The API has been carefully developed to make sure that the profile responses to users only gives the necessary data to that user, being the information SantaPone has always given in emails in past events.

    What about after the event?

    After the Secret Santa Event, I plan to remove all the user data once SantaPone has verified they have gotten any data they need for archival purposes for things like keeping track of grinches, or any other information needed. Auth0 Accounts will also be deleted, and the data service will be brought offline until the need for the next event.
